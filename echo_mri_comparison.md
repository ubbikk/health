## 5. Analysis & Conclusions

### How the 26.02.2026 echo compares to your healthy baseline

**Simpson EF = 55%** is abnormal for you. Here's the comparison:

| Context | Simpson EF | How 26.02.2026 compares |
|---|:---:|---|
| Your myocarditis-free range (12 echoes) | **59–68%** | 55% is **4 points below** your lowest healthy value |
| ESC lower limit of normal | 56% | 55% is **below normal** |
| Your most recent healthy echo (25.11.2025) | 60% | **Drop of 5 points** in 3 months |
| Best post-recovery (07.10.2025) | 67% | **Drop of 12 points** |
| 2nd myocarditis echo (18.01.2025) | 57% | **Very similar** (55% vs 57%) — and that echo was 3 days before MRI confirmed active myocarditis |
| 1st myocarditis echo (10.03.2024) | 39% | Not as severe |

**The Simpson–Teicholz discrepancy is also notable.** Teicholz = 61% while Simpson = 55% — a 6-point gap. In your healthy echoes, the two methods typically track within 1–5 points. This discrepancy can occur when there are regional wall motion abnormalities that Simpson's biplane tracing captures but Teicholz's single-dimension M-mode misses.

**КДО = 86 ml is the lowest in your entire echo history** (prior range: 88–140 ml). This likely reflects reduced preload from the recent illness (dehydration, reduced intake) rather than an intrinsic cardiac change. Low preload can itself reduce measured EF slightly, but probably not enough to fully explain the drop from 60% to 55%.

### What the full dataset shows

1. **Two confirmed myocarditis episodes** with a consistent pattern:
   - 1st episode (Mar 2024): EF dropped to 39% (echo) / 53% (MRI), recovered to 66–68% within 2 months
   - 2nd episode (Jan 2025): EF dropped to 57% (echo) / 46% (MRI), recovered to 59–67% within 2–3 months
   - Both times: subepi/mesocardial edema + LGE in the same distribution (IVS, inferior, lateral walls)
   - Both times: preceded by infection/inflammation

2. **Residual findings that persist even when myocarditis-free:**
   - LGE (myocardiofibrosis) — present on ALL MRIs, non-ischemic pattern, segments 2, 5, 6, 8, 11, 12 most involved
   - Chronic/resolving pericarditis — noted on all Omega MRIs through Aug 2025
   - MRI EF stabilized at 55–60% (consistently slightly lower than echo EF of 59–67%)

3. **The established pattern before each episode:**
   - Infection/inflammation → gradual cardiac involvement → EF drop on echo → MRI confirms active myocarditis
   - Critically: the echo on 18.01.2025 showed Simpson EF 57% (only mildly reduced), but the MRI 3 days later revealed EF 46% with active edema. **Echo can underestimate the degree of cardiac involvement.**
